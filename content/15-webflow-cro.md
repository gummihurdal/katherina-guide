# Webflow & conversion optimization

The second half of what Christa sells. Webflow for high-converting landing pages and brand sites. **CRO** (Conversion Rate Optimization) for making existing sites earn more.

Both are skills you can charge premium rates for — and they pair beautifully with Shopify, because most D2C brands use *Shopify for the store + Webflow for the marketing site.*

## Webflow — the designer's CMS

Webflow is what Shopify is for products, but for *marketing sites and landing pages*. It's a visual builder that produces real, clean HTML/CSS — not a no-code black box. Designers love it because they can ship without a developer. Performance marketers love it because they can launch a landing page for a new ad campaign in a day.

### Why D2C brands use Webflow alongside Shopify

- The Shopify storefront is for *buying*. Optimized for conversion-to-purchase.
- The Webflow site is for *telling the brand story*. Beautiful long pages, blogs, founder stories, press, careers.
- Marketing teams can update Webflow without touching the store code (safer).
- Webflow's CMS is much nicer than Shopify's for non-product content.

A common setup: `brand.com` runs on Webflow → "Shop" links go to `shop.brand.com` which runs on Shopify. You as the freelancer build and maintain both.

### What to learn in Webflow (in order)

1. **The Designer** (Webflow's visual editor). Spend a weekend in their free university courses at university.webflow.com. They are excellent — the best free tutorials in the industry.
2. **The box model** — Webflow forces you to actually understand CSS layout. This is good for you.
3. **CMS Collections** — Webflow's database. For blogs, case studies, team pages.
4. **Interactions & animations** — Webflow's animation tool is genuinely powerful. Scroll-triggered reveals, hover states, parallax. Used well, it's what makes sites feel *premium*.
5. **Figma-to-Webflow** — same workflow as Figma-to-Shopify. Designers hand you Figma, you produce a Webflow site that matches.

A polished Webflow landing page for a D2C brand typically pays **€1,500–€4,000**. Two weeks of work. You can get to commercially useful in about a month of evenings.

### Webflow's secret superpower for freelancers

The merchant gets a beautiful CMS to edit text and images themselves *after you hand it over.* That means:

- You build it once
- You charge a one-time fee + a small monthly maintenance retainer (€150–€400)
- The client edits their own copy
- You only get called for bigger changes

Ten retainer clients at €250/month is €30,000/year of recurring revenue, on top of new project fees. This is the actual freelance business.

## Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO)

This is the most underrated skill in the entire freelance market. CRO is *the discipline of making a site convert more visitors into buyers* — by changing layout, copy, images, and flow based on data.

### Why CRO pays so well

Imagine a D2C brand doing €100,000/month in revenue with a 2% conversion rate. If you improve their conversion to 2.5% — a tiny change — they make an extra €25,000/month. **Every month. Forever.**

How much would you charge to deliver that? €3,000? €5,000? €10,000? All cheap, relative to the value created.

That's why CRO consultants in Europe charge €150–€300/hour. They demonstrably make their clients more money than they cost.

### The CRO playbook (real, used by working consultants)

A CRO engagement looks like this:

**1. Install analytics that actually show you behavior.**
- Plausible or Google Analytics for traffic
- A heatmap tool (Hotjar, Microsoft Clarity — Clarity is free!) for where people click and scroll
- Session recordings to literally watch what users do on the site

**2. Find the leaks.** Common D2C leaks:
- Page loads in 5 seconds → 40% of users leave before it even loads
- Hero section unclear → users don't know what's being sold
- "Add to cart" button below the fold on mobile
- Checkout has 5 steps when it could have 2
- No social proof above the fold
- Shipping cost only revealed at the very end of checkout

**3. Hypothesize the biggest leak.** *"If we put the price and shipping info on the product page hero, conversion rate will increase because users won't bounce at checkout."*

**4. A/B test it.** Tools like Google Optimize successor (now mostly self-built with feature flags), Convert.com, VWO. Or simpler: just ship the change and measure week-over-week.

**5. Report the results.** Show before/after. Be honest when something didn't work — that's how you build trust.

### What you sell as a CRO consultant

Two packages:

**Package A — Audit (one-time, €2,000–€5,000)**
- Heatmap + session recording setup
- 90-minute interview with the founder
- A written report with the top 10 fixes ranked by expected impact
- A Loom video walkthrough

**Package B — Ongoing optimization (monthly retainer, €2,000–€5,000/month)**
- Implement the fixes from the audit
- Run experiments every 2–4 weeks
- Monthly report with numbers and next hypotheses
- Best with clients doing €50k+/month in revenue (so improvements actually matter)

### The skills under CRO

To be useful at CRO you need:

- **Reading data** (basic — Google Analytics, Plausible)
- **UX judgment** — knowing what's likely to confuse a buyer
- **Copywriting taste** — most "design" fixes are actually copy fixes
- **Light statistics** — understanding that 1 week with 100 visitors is not a real A/B test
- **Patience** — most experiments fail. The winning ones pay for the losing ones, many times over.

You will not be a great CRO consultant in three months. But you can offer a *€1,500 audit package* in three months, and learn on real clients while getting paid. That's the path.
