# Your first month

A real plan. Concrete enough to actually do. Loose enough to fit around school.

## Week 1 — Get your hands dirty

**Goal:** Stop being scared of Lovable.

- Day 1: Sign up. Build the portfolio from Chapter 2. Publish it. Send me the link.
- Day 2: Build a fake dog-walking business landing page. Make it ugly. That's fine.
- Day 3: Build a band page for the imaginary indie band Mandero (named after Grace's horse).
- Day 4: Take one of the three sites above and use the "second-pass" prompt from Chapter 3. Watch it get better.
- Day 5–7: Rest, read, or build a fourth throwaway. Just for fun.

**End-of-week check:** Have you published *something* publicly? If yes, you're ahead of schedule.

---

## Week 2 — Pick a real product, build the skeleton

**Goal:** Choose a real idea and get login + database working.

- Day 1: Re-read Chapter 9 ("Five things you can sell"). Pick **one**. Don't second-guess. Lock it in.
- Day 2: Plan the product. Use the "plan first" prompt from Chapter 3. Edit the plan until it's tight.
- Day 3: Build the landing page only. Just the pitch. No app yet.
- Day 4: Show the landing page to three people who fit the target customer. Watch their faces. Note what confused them.
- Day 5: Connect Supabase. Add user signup and login. (Chapter 5.)
- Day 6: Add the database tables. Turn on Row Level Security on every one.
- Day 7: Add the first "core feature" that makes this app actually useful.

**End-of-week check:** Can you sign up for your own product, log in, and use the main feature? If yes, you have an MVP.

---

## Week 3 — Make it sellable

**Goal:** Polish, payments, real domain.

- Day 1: Run the "second-pass" design prompt. Then again. Make it look like a designer made it.
- Day 2: Add Stripe Checkout. Test mode only. Two tiers (Free + Pro).
- Day 3: Add one AI-powered feature using the Claude API. Just one. Make it really good.
- Day 4: Add email sending via Resend — at minimum a welcome email when someone signs up.
- Day 5: Buy a custom domain at Cloudflare (~€10). Wire it up.
- Day 6: Generate legal pages — Privacy Policy, Terms, Impressum. Link them in the footer.
- Day 7: Go through the launch checklist in Chapter 8. Fix anything that fails.

**End-of-week check:** Is this something you'd be willing to charge a stranger €15 for? If yes → next week. If not → which week's missing day are you skipping? Go back and do it.

---

## Week 4 — Find your first customer

**Goal:** One human pays you. Any amount.

- Day 1: List 20 people in your phone who might want this. (Yes, twenty.)
- Day 2: Pick the 5 most likely. Record a 90-second Loom for each. Send them via WhatsApp or email.
- Day 3: Don't pester. Just wait. Build something fun while you wait. A throwaway side project.
- Day 4: Follow up *once*, gently, with anyone who didn't reply.
- Day 5: If you have any "interested but…" responses, get on a 15-minute call. Listen more than you talk. Find out the real objection.
- Day 6: Switch Stripe to **live mode**. Test once with a real card (your own). Refund yourself.
- Day 7: Take payment from the first person who says yes. Even if it's €5. Even if it's your dad. **The first sale is the proof.**

**End-of-month check:** Did any human give you any real money for something you built?

If yes — you've done what 99% of "I want to learn to code" people never do.

If not — what's the smallest thing you could change to get a yes next week? Do that thing. Then ask one more person.

---

## After the first month

Three options:

1. **Make this product bigger.** Add features, find more customers, raise the price.
2. **Make a second product** in a different niche, using everything you learned the first time.
3. **Take a break.** Read. Ride. Spend time with Mom or with friends. Then come back fresh.

All three are correct. There's no right answer. The wrong answer is staying in the same week forever.

I'll be here either way.
