# Customize a theme — without code

Your fake store works. It also looks like every other generic Shopify store, because you haven't touched the design yet. This chapter fixes that — using zero code. Everything happens in the visual theme editor.

By the end of this chapter, your store will look like *your* brand, not Shopify's default.

## Open the theme editor

In your admin: **Online Store → Themes → Customize** (the button next to your current theme).

A new screen opens, split in two:
- **Left side:** a list of pages and sections you can edit
- **Right side:** a live preview of your store

This is the theme editor. It's the single most-used tool in a Shopify freelancer's day. You'll spend hundreds of hours here.

## The core concept: sections

A **section** is one horizontal stripe of your homepage. Every Shopify homepage is just sections stacked on top of each other.

Look at your store right now. You'll see something like:

1. **Header** (logo + menu — at the top)
2. **Image banner** (the big hero image)
3. **Featured collection** (a row of products)
4. **Image with text** (some words plus a photo)
5. **Newsletter** (a sign-up form)
6. **Footer**

Each of those is a section. Each one can be:

- **Edited** — change its text, images, colors
- **Moved up or down** — drag-and-drop in the editor
- **Hidden** — toggle off without deleting
- **Duplicated** — make a copy with different content
- **Deleted** — remove entirely

**This is the magic of Shopify.** Once you set up sections well, your client can rearrange their homepage themselves. You build the structure once and they manage the content forever. (That's how Christa keeps retainers profitable.)

## Customize the hero section

Click the **Image banner** section in the left panel. The right panel zooms to that section. You'll see settings like:

- **Heading** — change it to *"Hand-picked. Hand-made. Hand-delivered."*
- **Subheading** — *"Tomatoes from Burgenland, sauce from grandma's kitchen."*
- **Button label** — *"Shop the sauces"*
- **Button link** — pick your products collection
- **Image** — upload a beautiful tomato photo

Click **Save** in the top-right.

Look at your store now. It looks *intentional*. That single section change is the difference between *"AI default"* and *"a brand."*

## Theme settings vs section settings

Two places to change things. Learn the difference:

**Theme settings** (gear icon at the bottom-left of the editor) — affect the *whole site*. Colors, fonts, button styles, logo. Change once, applies everywhere.

**Section settings** (click any section) — affect *just that section*. Heading, text, alignment, padding.

A common beginner mistake: trying to change the brand color section-by-section. Don't. Open **Theme settings → Colors** and change the brand color in *one* place. The whole site updates.

## A 20-minute upgrade exercise

Spend 20 minutes turning your fake tomato sauce store into something you'd actually buy from. Do these in order:

1. **Theme settings → Typography:** swap to a more brand-appropriate font pair. Try *Fraunces* + *Inter* or *Playfair Display* + *DM Sans*.
2. **Theme settings → Colors:** pick three colors max. A warm cream background, a deep red, a soft sage.
3. **Header:** upload a logo (even just text — Shopify makes a logo from your store name automatically).
4. **Hero section:** strong photo + clear headline + one button.
5. **Add a "Multicolumn" section** below the hero with 3 icons + short text: *"Hand-picked," "Slow-cooked," "Glass-bottled."*
6. **Featured collection:** point it at your fake products.
7. **Image with text section:** brand story. *"Our family has been making sauce in Burgenland for three generations."*
8. **Mobile preview button** (top of editor): check everything works on a phone-width screen.

Click **Save.** Look at the result. It went from generic to brand in under half an hour.

## Mobile is not optional

About 70% of D2C traffic is mobile. The mobile preview button (top of the editor, looks like a phone) is your most-used button.

A site that looks great on desktop but breaks on mobile **is broken.** Always check both.

## What the theme editor can't do

After a few hours in the theme editor, you'll hit its limits. Common things you can't do without code:

- Add a *new type* of section (you can rearrange existing ones, not invent new ones)
- Change the layout of the product page in ways the theme doesn't allow
- Add custom animations
- Pull in data from a custom source
- Build a unique cart drawer
- Anything the theme designer didn't expect

The moment you hit that wall, it's time to learn Liquid. Next chapter.
