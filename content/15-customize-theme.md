# Woche 2 — Customize a theme, no code

You have a working fake store. It still looks like every other Shopify store on the planet. This week we fix that — without writing a line of code. Everything happens in the visual theme editor.

By the end you'll be able to take *any* brand brief and rebuild a generic Shopify theme into something brand-specific in under an hour. That's a sellable skill on its own.

Plan: **4–5 hours total**, split across two evenings.

---

## Übung 1 — Read three real stores like a developer (20 min)

**Deliverable:** a one-page section map for three real D2C stores.

This is how pros learn taste. Before you customize anything, study three real D2C sites that you admire.

Pick three Austrian/DACH D2C brands (study Christa's clients for inspiration — but pick different ones to avoid mimicking). For each one:

1. Open the homepage on your laptop
2. Scroll slowly from top to bottom
3. **Make a list of every section** you see. Like this:

```
[brand-name].com  —  homepage section map
1. Sticky header with announcement bar
2. Full-bleed hero with video background + 2-line headline + 1 button
3. Logo row of press mentions
4. 3-column "icon + heading + text" benefits
5. Product carousel ("Bestsellers")
6. Brand story — image left, text right
7. Testimonial slider with customer photos
8. Instagram feed
9. Newsletter sign-up
10. Footer with 4 columns
```

Do this for three stores. **Save the three maps in a notes file.** You'll reference them throughout this chapter.

This exercise alone — done five times — gives you better D2C taste than three weeks of theory.

✅ Stop when you have three section maps written down.

---

## Übung 2 — Open the theme editor (5 min)

**Deliverable:** comfort with the editor's layout.

1. Admin → **Online Store → Themes → Customize**
2. Spend 3 minutes clicking around. Notice:
   - **Left panel:** sections of the current page, in order
   - **Right panel:** live preview
   - **Top bar:** page selector (Home / Product / Cart / Collection / etc.) — switch between them
   - **Top-right:** Save button, mobile/desktop preview toggle
   - **Bottom-left:** Theme settings (gear icon) — affects the whole site
3. Click the **mobile preview** toggle. The right panel shrinks to phone-width. **Get used to this view — it's your most important view.**

✅ Stop when you can switch between Home, Product, and Collection pages and between desktop and mobile preview without thinking.

---

## Übung 3 — Theme settings → brand foundation (25 min)

**Deliverable:** colours, fonts, and logo set up for your brand.

This is the foundational layer. Get it right once and every section inherits it.

1. **Theme settings (gear icon) → Colors**
   - Define a *brand color* (the dominant accent)
   - A *text color* (almost-black, not pure black — try `#1A1F2E` or `#2B2A28`)
   - A *background color* (a warm off-white, not pure white — try `#FAF7F2` or `#F4F0E8`)
   - A *button background* and *button text* color
2. **Theme settings → Typography**
   - **Heading font:** Fraunces (or Playfair Display, or Instrument Serif)
   - **Body font:** Inter (or DM Sans, or Geist)
   - Test that they're set up by saving and looking at your store
3. **Theme settings → Logo**
   - Either upload a logo, or use the store name as a "wordmark"
   - Tip: if you don't have a logo, just set the font of your text logo to match your heading font — that's a perfectly good first version
4. **Theme settings → Favicon** — upload a small 32×32 icon. Even a single letter is fine.

**Save.** Visit your store. You'll see the colour and font choices applied across every page automatically. This is the power of theme settings — change once, applies everywhere.

✅ Stop when your store visibly has *your brand's* colours and fonts on every page, including the product page and the cart.

---

## Übung 4 — Build the hero, three times (45 min)

**Deliverable:** three versions of the same hero section, screenshotted side by side.

The hero is the first thing every visitor sees. It's the section that matters most. Practice building it until it's automatic.

For each version, edit the **Image banner** section (or whatever your theme calls it):

**Version A — Editorial.** Big serif headline, lots of whitespace, no background image — just a colour. Single CTA button.

> *"Müesli, slow-made. Eight grains, no sugar."* → button: *"See the range"*

**Version B — Photo-first.** Full-bleed product photo as background, white text overlay, single button. Bold and confident.

> *"Made by hand in Oberösterreich."* → button: *"Shop the Apfel-Walnuss"*

**Version C — Two-up.** Image left, text right (use a "split" or "two-column" layout if your theme has one — or use the Image with text section instead of Image banner). Smaller headline, longer description.

Take screenshots of all three. Compare them side by side. **Which one feels most like your brand?** Why?

**The lesson:** the hero you ship is the result of trying 3–5 versions, not the first one that came out. This is the *kerngewerbe* — the core craft. Get used to building 3 variants of everything.

✅ Stop when you have three screenshotted hero variants.

---

## Übung 5 — The full homepage (60 min)

**Deliverable:** a complete homepage with 7+ sections, ordered intentionally.

Open one of your three section maps from Übung 1. Use it as a blueprint. Build a homepage with these sections, in this order:

1. **Image banner** (hero) — your favourite variant from Übung 4
2. **Multicolumn** — 3 icons + 3-word benefit + 1-line description. (*"Hand-picked / No sugar / Compostable packaging"*)
3. **Featured collection** — point at your "Bestsellers"
4. **Image with text** — brand story, image left, paragraph right
5. **Featured product** — highlight one hero product with its variants
6. **Testimonials** (if the theme has a multi-column section, use that with 3 quotes)
7. **Newsletter** — at the bottom
8. **Footer** — already there

For each section, fill it with **real-feeling content.** Not lorem ipsum. Not "Headline." Write copy as if it were going live tomorrow.

When done, click **Save** and **View** your store. Walk through it as a customer. Does it feel cohesive? Does each section earn its place?

Take a screenshot of the whole page (use Firefox's "save screenshot full page" or a Chrome extension like GoFullPage).

✅ Stop when you have a complete, screenshotted homepage that feels like a real brand.

---

## Übung 6 — The product page (30 min)

**Deliverable:** a customized product page that converts.

Switch to the **Product** page in the top selector. Most themes let you customize this too — sections, order, what's shown.

1. Make sure these are present and in this order:
   - Product image gallery
   - Title + price + variant selector
   - "Add to cart" button — **above the fold on mobile** (toggle to mobile and verify)
   - 2–3 lines of selling copy (this is *not* the long description — it's the hook)
   - Free shipping over €50 notice
   - Long description below
   - **Trust badges** if your theme has them (often a "Multicolumn" section: returns / shipping / made-in-Austria)
   - Related products
2. Pick one of your products and view it. Try it on mobile. **Does the Add to cart button appear before any scrolling?** If not, fix the order.

The product page is where 80% of conversions happen. Getting it right matters more than the homepage.

✅ Stop when the Add to cart button is visible without scrolling on mobile.

---

## Übung 7 — The Doppelgänger drill (90 min)

**Deliverable:** a homepage that visually matches a real D2C brand from memory.

This is the apprenticeship technique that produces taste fastest: copy a master.

1. Pick **one** of the three real brands you analysed in Übung 1.
2. Open it in one tab. Open your theme editor in another.
3. **Without using code,** make your store homepage look as close to theirs as you can. Same section order. Same vibe. Similar fonts (use your closest Google Fonts match). Similar palette.
4. **Time yourself.** Set a 90-minute timer.

When time's up, screenshot your version next to theirs. Compare them. Where are the gaps?

**This drill is uncomfortable.** You'll feel like a copy-cat. You're not — every designer learns this way. You're not selling this; you're training your eye. After three Doppelgänger drills with three different brands, your default Shopify output looks 5x better.

✅ Stop when the 90 minutes are up. Save both screenshots in your portfolio folder labeled `doppelgaenger-01-[brandname]`.

## Lehrling Notiz

You'll start to feel the limits of the theme editor in Übung 7 — *"I wish I could move that line three pixels left,"* or *"this section won't let me add a fourth column."* That feeling is the green light to start learning code, which is next week.

Don't fight the editor. Just notice where it stops you. Those notes are your next-week curriculum.
