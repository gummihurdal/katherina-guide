# Five things you can actually sell

Pick **one**. Build it end-to-end. Sell it. The first sale is the magic moment.

The rule: don't build for an imaginary audience. Build for *one specific person you know* who has the problem. Watch them use it. Charge them. Then find five more people like them.

---

## 1. Local business in a box

**Price:** €500–€1,500 per site

A polished one-page website for a single business: hairdresser, physiotherapist, café, yoga studio, dentist, dog groomer. Hero, services with prices, about, contact form, Google Maps embed, opening hours, photo gallery. Custom domain.

**Why it pays:** Every small business in Austria needs this. Most have terrible sites — broken on mobile, six years out of date, written in MS FrontPage. Walk into 10 cafés in Baden, point out their site is broken on a phone screen, offer to fix it for €500. One will say yes.

**What you sell:** the site + custom domain + 6 months of small updates. Then renewal at €15/month for hosting and small changes.

---

## 2. Booking SaaS for one niche

**Price:** €19–€39/month per business

A booking page for a *specific* niche: tutors, dog walkers, personal trainers, language teachers, music instructors. Customers book slots, pay a deposit upfront, get reminder emails. You charge the *provider* a monthly fee.

**Why it pays:** It replaces Calendly + Stripe + reminder emails + spreadsheets, all in one. Niche-specific beats generic — *"Calendly for piano teachers"* outsells *"Calendly but cheaper"* every time.

**Bonus moat:** add one feature only your niche cares about (for piano teachers: progress tracking; for dog walkers: GPS tracking on walks).

---

## 3. The AI niche tool

**Price:** €9–€29/month, or €4 per use

A single-purpose AI tool. *Examples:*

- AI cover letter writer specifically for the Austrian job market (knows the conventions)
- AI grading rubric generator for teachers (HS, AHS, BHS — Austrian school types)
- AI lease-letter translator for landlords renting to international tenants
- AI menu translator for restaurants (German → English/Italian/Mandarin)

**Why it pays:** People don't pay for "ChatGPT but…" — they pay for *"ChatGPT preconfigured for my exact annoying task that I do 12 times a month."*

The tighter the niche, the more you can charge. *"AI menu translator"* is worth more than *"AI translator."*

---

## 4. The micro-directory

**Price:** Free to browse → €50–€500/month from featured listings and ads

A focused directory people genuinely search for:

- "Best vegetarian restaurants in Vienna"
- "Yoga retreats in the Alps"
- "L17 driving schools in Austria"
- "Coworking spaces with childcare in DACH"

Static-ish site + a database of listings. Free to browse, businesses pay to be featured or to claim/update their listing.

**Why it pays:** Google sends *free* traffic if you actually have the best directory for a specific thing. Once you rank, money trickles in for years with almost no maintenance.

---

## 5. The data-pull dashboard

**Price:** €49–€199/month per customer

Connect to a public API (or a business's own data) and turn it into a beautiful dashboard.

- Instagram + TikTok analytics for small influencers
- Strava insights for cycling clubs
- School grade trackers for parents (Austrian *Klassenbuch* integration)
- Local-weather + tide + wind dashboards for boat-rental businesses

**Why it pays:** Boring data is everywhere; beautiful, focused dashboards are rare. You're not building intelligence — you're building *taste*. AI helps with the building. Your job is picking the right boring data to make beautiful.

---

## How to pick

Go through this list and ask yourself, for each one: *"Do I personally know someone with this problem?"*

If yes → that's your first idea. If no → skip it.

The single biggest mistake first-time founders make is building for a customer they've never met. You have 17 years of social network in Austria. **Use it.**

The right product is the one where your first three customers are already in your phone contacts.
