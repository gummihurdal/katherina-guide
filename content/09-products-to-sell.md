# Werkzeugkasten — Sellable patterns

A reference chapter, not a weekly Lehrling. After your first SaaS, you'll want a second one. Here are five reusable patterns, with what they cost to build and what they earn.

## How to read this chapter

Each pattern below lists:

- **What it is** — one-line summary
- **Who pays for it** — the customer profile
- **What it earns** — realistic monthly revenue range
- **Build time** — weeks of focused work using the Lehre 1 stack
- **What's hard about it** — the honest catch

Pick patterns that match a real person you know who has the problem. That's the cheat code for first-customer-fast.

---

## Pattern 1 — Local business in a box

**What it is.** A polished one-page website for a single small business: hairdresser, physiotherapist, café, yoga studio, dog groomer, dentist. Hero + services with prices + about + contact form + map + opening hours + photo gallery + custom domain.

**Who pays for it.** Owners of small Austrian/Swiss/German businesses whose current site is broken on mobile. They are everywhere. Walk down any street in Baden, Linz, Zurich.

**What it earns.** One-time **€500–€1,500 per site**, then **€15–€30/month maintenance** for hosting, small updates, copy changes.

**Build time.** 2 weekends of work per site, once you've built one.

**What's hard.** Sales, not building. You have to walk into actual businesses. Most owners will say no. One in ten will say yes.

**Why pick it.** Lowest barrier to entry. First customer can be a real café you go to. Compounds via referrals.

## Pattern 2 — Booking SaaS for one niche

**What it is.** A booking page for a *specific* niche: tutors, dog walkers, personal trainers, language teachers, music instructors. Customers book slots, pay a deposit upfront, get reminder emails. You charge the *provider* a monthly fee.

**Who pays for it.** Self-employed providers in one niche. The narrower the niche, the better.

**What it earns.** **€19–€39/month per provider.** Get 50 providers, that's €1,000–€2,000/month recurring.

**Build time.** 4–6 weeks for v1.

**What's hard.** Niche selection. "Generic booking app" loses to Calendly. "Booking app for piano teachers in DACH" wins. Find your niche before building.

**Why pick it.** TutorBuch (Option B in Lehre 1) is exactly this. You're closer to this than you think.

## Pattern 3 — AI niche tool

**What it is.** A single-purpose AI tool for one specific annoying task. Examples:

- AI cover letter writer specifically for the Austrian job market (knows the format conventions)
- AI grading rubric generator for teachers (HS/AHS/BHS Austrian school types)
- AI translation between formal/informal German for emails
- AI menu translator for restaurants (German → English/Italian/Mandarin)
- AI study summarizer for Austrian *Matura* prep

**Who pays for it.** People who do this annoying task often enough to feel the pain monthly.

**What it earns.** **€9–€29/month subscription** or **€4–€19 per use** (one-shot purchase).

**Build time.** 1–2 weeks. AI niche tools are fast to ship because most of the value is in the system prompt, not the code.

**What's hard.** Distribution. The product is easy. Getting 100 people to discover it is the actual work.

**Why pick it.** Highest skill-to-revenue ratio. Once you have your first AI tool live, the second one takes a long weekend.

## Pattern 4 — The micro-directory

**What it is.** A focused directory people Google for. Static-ish site + a small database of listings. Free to browse. Businesses pay to be featured, claimed, or to update their listing.

Examples that work:

- *"Best vegetarian restaurants in Vienna"*
- *"Yoga retreats in the Alps"*
- *"L17 driving schools in Austria"*
- *"Coworking spaces with childcare in DACH"*
- *"Best second-hand clothing shops in Salzburg"*

**Who pays for it.** Listed businesses (pay for premium placement), or readers via affiliate links.

**What it earns.** **€50–€500/month** from listings, plus ad/affiliate revenue if you get traffic.

**Build time.** 1 week for the site, **months for the SEO** to actually rank.

**What's hard.** SEO takes patience. You ship in week 1, you don't see revenue until month 6.

**Why pick it.** Highly defensible once ranked. Income comes in while you sleep. Great as a portfolio-piece-that-also-pays.

## Pattern 5 — Data-pull dashboard

**What it is.** Connect to a public API (or a business's data) and turn it into a beautiful focused dashboard. Examples:

- Instagram + TikTok analytics for small influencers
- Strava insights for cycling clubs
- School grade trackers for Austrian parents (with *Klassenbuch* integration)
- Weather + tide + wind for boat-rental businesses
- Hafnia investor dashboard (you know one)

**Who pays for it.** Anyone whose decisions could be better with a daily-glance dashboard.

**What it earns.** **€49–€199/month per customer.**

**Build time.** 2–3 weeks, depending on API complexity.

**What's hard.** APIs change. You'll patch breakage every few months. Worth the price you charge.

**Why pick it.** Boring data is everywhere. Beautiful focused dashboards are rare. The premium is in the taste, not the tech.

## How to pick

Read all five. For each, ask yourself one question:

**"Do I personally know someone with this problem?"**

If yes → that's your first idea. If no → skip it.

The single biggest mistake first-time founders make is building for a customer they've never met. You have 17 years of social network in Austria. The right product is the one where your first three customers are already in your phone contacts.

## A small Übung

**Übung — Map your network to patterns (30 min)**

In a notes file, list 10 people you know who run something — a small business, a side project, a school, a club. For each, ask: would any of the 5 patterns above help them?

You'll be surprised. Half your contacts already need one of these. The right move is sending one of them a Loom showing the version you'd build, *for them, for free, for 30 days.*

Save the file as `lehre-1/network-map.md`.

✅ Done when 10 contacts are mapped to patterns.
