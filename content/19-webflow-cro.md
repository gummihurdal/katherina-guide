# Webflow & conversion optimization

The second half of what Christa sells. **Webflow** for high-converting landing pages and brand sites. **CRO** (Conversion Rate Optimization) for making existing sites earn more.

Both pair beautifully with Shopify, because most D2C brands run *Shopify for the store + Webflow for the marketing site* and need someone who handles both.

## Webflow — the designer's CMS

Webflow is the most polished visual web builder on the market. Unlike Shopify (which is for selling) and Lovable (which is for everything), Webflow is specifically for **beautiful marketing sites and landing pages.**

It produces real, clean HTML/CSS — not a no-code black box. Designers love it because they can ship without a developer. Performance marketers love it because they can launch a landing page for a new ad campaign in a day.

### The Shopify + Webflow combo

A typical D2C brand setup:

- **`brand.com`** — runs on Webflow. Brand story, founder, blog, careers, press, *long-form story-telling.*
- **`brand.com/shop`** or `shop.brand.com` — links to Shopify. Products, cart, checkout, transactional.

Why split it? Because Shopify's editor is built for product pages, not for editorial content. And Webflow's CMS is way nicer for blogs and case studies than Shopify's pages.

You as the freelancer build and maintain both. Two retainers from the same brand.

### What to learn in Webflow (in order)

1. **The Designer** — Webflow's visual editor. Spend a weekend in their free courses at **university.webflow.com**. They are genuinely excellent, the best free tutorials in the industry. Don't pay for anything else until you've finished these.

2. **The box model** — Webflow forces you to actually understand CSS layout (flex, grid, padding, margin). This makes you a better developer in every other context too.

3. **CMS Collections** — Webflow's database. For blogs, case studies, team pages, anything repeating. Conceptually the same as a Shopify product collection.

4. **Interactions & animations** — Webflow's animation tool is genuinely powerful. Scroll-triggered reveals, hover states, parallax, page-load sequences. Used well, this is what makes a site feel *premium*. Used badly, it's seasickness.

5. **Figma-to-Webflow** — same workflow as Figma-to-Shopify (chapter 18). Designers hand you Figma, you produce a Webflow site that matches. Slightly easier than Shopify because Webflow's editor is more design-aware.

A polished Webflow landing page for a D2C brand typically pays **€1,500–€4,000**. Two weeks of work. Commercial useful in about a month of evenings.

## Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO)

The most underrated skill in the entire freelance market. CRO is *the discipline of making a site convert more visitors into buyers* — by changing layout, copy, images, flow, and pricing based on data.

### Why CRO pays so well

Imagine a D2C brand doing **€100,000/month in revenue** with a 2% conversion rate. If you improve their conversion to 2.5% — a tiny change — they make an extra **€25,000/month. Every month. Forever.**

How much would you charge to deliver that? €3,000? €5,000? €10,000? All cheap, relative to the value created.

That's why CRO consultants in Europe charge €150–€300/hour. They demonstrably make their clients more money than they cost.

### The CRO playbook (real, used by working consultants)

A CRO engagement looks like this:

**1. Install analytics that actually show you behaviour.**
- **Plausible** or **Google Analytics** for traffic and basic conversion funnels
- **Microsoft Clarity** for heatmaps and session recordings — and it's free, no excuses
- **Hotjar** is the paid alternative (€32/month) — slightly better UX but Clarity is fine

**2. Find the leaks.** Common D2C leaks you'll see again and again:

- Page loads in 5 seconds → 40% of users leave before it loads
- Hero section unclear → users don't know what's being sold in the first 3 seconds
- "Add to cart" button below the fold on mobile
- Checkout has 5 steps when it could have 2
- No social proof above the fold (reviews, press mentions, customer photos)
- Shipping cost only revealed at the very end of checkout
- Search broken or hard to find
- Product images small, low-resolution, or in a bad order
- No clear "best seller" or recommendation

**3. Hypothesise the biggest leak.** *"If we put shipping info on the product page hero, conversion rate will increase because users won't bounce at checkout when they see the shipping cost."*

**4. A/B test it.** Tools: **Convert.com**, **VWO**, or simpler: just ship the change for a week, measure, compare to the previous week.

**5. Report results.** Show before/after numbers. **Be honest when something didn't work** — that's how you build trust. Bad consultants only report wins. Good ones report everything.

### What you sell as a CRO consultant

Two packages:

**Audit (one-time, €2,000–€5,000)**
- Microsoft Clarity + session recording setup
- 90-minute interview with the founder about their customers
- A written report with the top 10 fixes ranked by expected impact
- A Loom video walkthrough of the report

**Ongoing optimisation (monthly retainer, €2,000–€5,000/month)**
- Implement the fixes from the audit
- Run one experiment every 2–4 weeks
- Monthly report with numbers and next hypotheses
- Best for clients doing **€50k+/month in revenue** — that's when 0.5% improvements actually pay your retainer back

### The skills under CRO

To be useful at CRO:

- **Reading data** — basic, Google Analytics, Plausible
- **UX judgment** — what's likely to confuse a buyer
- **Copywriting taste** — most "design" fixes are actually copy fixes
- **Light statistics** — understanding that 1 week with 100 visitors isn't a real A/B test
- **Patience** — most experiments fail. The winning ones pay for the losing ones, many times over.

You will not be a great CRO consultant in three months. But you can offer a **€1,500 audit package** in three months, and learn on real clients while getting paid. That's the path.
