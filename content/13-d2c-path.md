# Level up — the D2C path

You've finished the first thirteen chapters. You've built something, shipped it, and ideally taken money from a human being. This is the part of the guide where the income ceiling lifts dramatically.

## Meet your benchmark: christa.dev

There's a freelancer in Vienna called **Christa Kietaibl**. One person, sometimes a tiny team. Eight years in. Sixty-plus brands shipped. Her site is at *christa.dev* — go read it before you continue this chapter.

What she sells:

1. **Shopify development** — new stores, theme work, Figma-to-Shopify, ongoing shop maintenance
2. **Webflow development** — high-converting landing pages and brand sites
3. **Conversion Rate Optimization** (CRO) — data-driven design to make existing sites earn more

What she doesn't sell: everything else. She's brutally focused. *"I deliberately specialize in Shopify and Webflow — that's how I can offer the best service."*

**That's the lesson. The whole lesson.** A specialist in one platform out-earns a generalist by 3–5x in this market. People don't pay a premium for "web developer." They pay a premium for *"the Shopify person in Vienna who does Figma-to-Shopify in two weeks for D2C brands."*

## Why this path is enormous

The D2C (direct-to-consumer) world is huge. Shopify alone powers about 4 million stores. Many of them belong to small brands that:

- Need a designer's eye but can't afford an agency
- Need fast updates but can't afford a full-time developer
- Care about conversion rate above all (one extra percentage point = thousands of euros)
- Want one human who's available, not a team of five who pass tickets around

That's the customer profile. They live in Vienna, Berlin, Zurich, Milan. There are *thousands* of them in DACH alone. Christa picked off 60. There's room for a thousand more Christas.

## What you can charge (the honest numbers)

These are real 2026 European D2C freelancer rates:

| Service | Realistic price |
|---|---|
| Small Shopify theme tweak (1–2 days) | €600–€1,200 |
| New landing page (Webflow or Shopify) | €1,500–€4,000 |
| Full Figma-to-Shopify build | €4,000–€12,000 |
| Monthly shop retainer (4–8 hours/week) | €1,500–€3,500/month |
| Performance / CRO audit + recommendations | €2,000–€5,000 |
| Hourly rate after a few years | €90–€180/hour |

A solo Vienna-based Shopify freelancer with three retainer clients makes more than a typical SNB IT salary. With no commute and no boss. That math is worth pausing on.

## What you actually have to learn

To do what Christa does, you need exactly four skills. Not ten. Four:

1. **Shopify Liquid + theme development** (next chapter)
2. **Webflow** for landing pages (chapter after that)
3. **Figma reading** — turning a designer's file into a working store
4. **Conversion Rate Optimization** — knowing which changes actually move money

Each skill takes roughly 2–4 weeks of focused learning to get *commercially useful* — meaning good enough to ship paid work. So in four to six months of patient evenings, you can do everything Christa does.

You will not be as good as her in six months. She has eight years of taste you can't shortcut. But you'll be good enough to take €1,500 retainers. That is the entire game.

## A small mental shift

The first part of this guide was about building *products you own*. SaaS, AI tools, dashboards. They scale beautifully but take years to grow.

This second part is about being *a person businesses hire*. It scales worse, but pays well from day one and never has a "is anyone going to buy this?" question. You're not gambling on a product — you're selling skill.

Both paths are valid. Smart people do both. They run a small SaaS in the background while taking 1–2 freelance clients to keep the lights on. That's the actual playbook.
