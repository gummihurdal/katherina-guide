# Your first website

The goal of this chapter is exactly one thing: **something live on the internet, today.**

Not perfect. Not amazing. Just *real.*

## Step by step

1. Open **lovable.dev**. Click "New project."

2. Paste this exact prompt into the chat:

> Build a one-page personal portfolio for a 17-year-old web developer named Katherina, based in Austria.
>
> Sections: hero with name and tagline "I build small useful things on the internet", an About paragraph, a Projects section with three placeholder cards, and a Contact section with an email button.
>
> Style: clean, modern, lots of whitespace, dark mode by default, accent color is warm coral (#FF6B6B). Use a serif font for headings and sans-serif for body text. Smooth scroll between sections.

3. Wait about 60 seconds. Look at the preview.

4. **Don't panic if it isn't perfect.** It almost never is on the first try. That's normal.

5. In the chat, type: *"Make the hero section taller and add a subtle gradient background from dark navy to almost-black."*

6. Wait another 30 seconds. Look again.

7. Click **Publish** in the top-right corner. Copy the link.

8. Send it to me.

## What just happened

You described a thing in plain English. A machine wrote ~500 lines of React, set up Tailwind for styling, built a working page, and put it on the internet. Total time: about 20 minutes.

This is what your dad couldn't do until he was 25, and it took him weeks. You did it before lunch.

## The instinct to fix

You'll feel a strong urge to immediately fix every imperfect detail. **Resist it for now.** Just publish what you have. The point of chapter 1 isn't perfection — it's the click of the Publish button and the realization that *there's nothing stopping you.*

The polish comes in chapters 3 and 4. Today, we just want the thing to exist.

## A small homework

Before chapter 3, build two more tiny throwaway sites. Don't try to make them good. Try to make them *fast*.

- A landing page for an imaginary dog-walking business in Vienna
- A page for an imaginary indie band called Mandero (yes, after the horse)

Each should take under 15 minutes. The point is to feel how Lovable responds — what works, what confuses it. You're not learning to code. You're learning a new conversation.
