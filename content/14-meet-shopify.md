# Meet Shopify

You've spent thirteen chapters building things from scratch with Lovable. Shopify is the opposite mindset. **You don't build a store — you customize a platform that already runs millions of them.**

This chapter has one job: get you signed up, looking at an admin panel, with one fake product live. No code. No theory. Just clicking.

## Why Shopify is different

Lovable gives you a blank page and helps you build whatever. Shopify gives you a complete online shop on day one — products, cart, checkout, payments, taxes, shipping, customer accounts — all already working. Your job is just to make it *look* like the right brand and *flow* the right way.

Think of it like this:

- **Lovable** = you build a custom car from parts
- **Shopify** = you take an existing car and customize the interior, paint, wheels

The car already drives. Your value is taste, not engineering.

## Sign up (3 minutes)

1. Go to **shopify.com** → click "Start free trial"
2. Email + password. That's it.
3. Shopify asks "what are you selling?" — pick anything. *"Skincare"* or *"jewelry"* are fine.
4. Skip every "set up your shipping" / "add tax" question. Click *"I'll do this later"* on everything.

You land in the Shopify admin. This is where you'll spend hundreds of hours over your career, so let's tour it.

## The five-minute admin tour

In the left sidebar you'll see roughly these sections. They never move. Memorize them:

- **Home** — overview of orders, traffic, etc. You'll mostly ignore this.
- **Orders** — when someone buys, the order shows up here.
- **Products** — every product you sell.
- **Customers** — people who bought from you.
- **Content** — pages, blog posts, menus.
- **Marketing** — automations, discount codes, gift cards.
- **Analytics** — sales reports, traffic.
- **Online Store** — this is the one you'll use most. Themes, pages, navigation, blogs.

The **"Online Store"** section is where the visual side of your job lives. Click into it now and look around — but don't change anything yet.

## Add your first fake product

Click **Products → Add product.**

Make up a brand. Try:

- Title: *"Findling Tomatensauce — 350ml glass jar"*
- Description: a few lines about how it's made with hand-picked Austrian tomatoes
- Price: €7.90
- Image: just drag any photo from your laptop
- Click **Save**

Now click *"View"* on the product. **You just made an actual e-commerce product page.** It has a buy button. It has a description. It has an image. The only reason it isn't selling is because no one knows it exists.

Add two more fake products so the store doesn't feel empty.

## See the store as a customer

In the left sidebar, click **Online Store → Themes → View your store.**

A new tab opens with your actual store. Browse it. Click your products. Try to add one to the cart. **You can.** Shopify already wired up the cart, the checkout, the payment form. All you did was list a product.

This is the gift Shopify gives you. The 80% of e-commerce that's the same for every shop — Shopify handles it. Your job is the 20% that makes a store feel like *this brand* and not *every brand*.

## Why this matters for your career

Most beginners spend months learning Shopify because they treat it like it's complicated. It isn't. Shopify is roughly five interfaces stacked together:

1. The **admin** (what you just toured)
2. The **theme editor** (visual customization — next chapter)
3. The **theme code** (where the real money is — chapter 16+)
4. The **app store** (third-party add-ons — touch lightly)
5. The **Shopify CLI** (pro-level local development — chapter 17)

Each one builds on the last. You're done with #1 now. Take a break. Make tea. Come back for #2.
